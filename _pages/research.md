## Research Overview

The Environmental Research Laboratory works at the intersection of **waste management**, **urban sustainability**, and **energy systems**. Our work spans quantitative modelling (life cycle assessment, techno-economic analysis), experimental work, and policy analysis — aiming to produce actionable science for cities and policymakers in India and beyond.

---

## Municipal Solid Waste Management & Life Cycle Assessment

India's cities generate over 150,000 tonnes of solid waste per day, much of which is still landfilled. Our research quantifies the environmental and economic performance of alternative MSW strategies — from source segregation and composting to waste-to-energy and landfill diversion — using LCA and life cycle costing (LCC).

**Key publications:**
- Life cycle assessment of potential MSW management strategies for Mumbai *(Resources, Conservation and Recycling, 2017)*
- Life cycle cost analysis of MSW management scenarios for Mumbai *(Waste Management, 2021)*
- Potential of waste-to-energy in reducing greenhouse gas emissions *(Carbon Management, 2012)*
- Future of flash graphene for sustainable management of solid waste in megacities *(ACS Nano, 2021)*

## Biogas Production & Organic Waste Valorisation

Organic waste — food scraps, market waste, sewage sludge — is a resource. We study anaerobic digestion and co-digestion to maximise biogas yields, exploring pretreatment methods (acid, alkali) and co-substrate synergies that can make decentralised biogas plants viable in Indian conditions.

**Key publications:**
- Enhancement of biogas production from organic fraction of MSW using acid pretreatment *(Biomass and Bioenergy, 2020)* — A. Dasgupta & M.K. Chandel
- Anaerobic co-digestion of sewage sludge and OFMSW for enhanced biogas production *(Renewable Energy, 2023)* — A. Kumari & M.K. Chandel
- Estimation of energy recovery potential of sewage sludge in India *(Bioresource Technology, 2020)*

## Wastewater & Water–Energy Nexus

Water treatment is energy-intensive; that energy has a carbon cost. We perform LCA and embodied-energy analysis of wastewater reuse alternatives, assessing trade-offs between water security, energy demand, and greenhouse gas emissions — with case studies in Mumbai and the Indian Himalayan region.

**Key publications:**
- Life cycle assessment of wastewater reuse alternatives *(Science of the Total Environment, 2024)* — R. Negi & M.K. Chandel
- Embodied energy and GHG emissions from wastewater reuse strategies in the Indian Himalayan region *(Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews, 2024)* — R. Negi & M.K. Chandel

## Urban Transport Emissions & Climate Adaptation

Transport is one of the largest and fastest-growing sources of urban GHG emissions in India. We model how congestion, rainfall, and infrastructure choices affect fuel consumption and emissions in the Mumbai Metropolitan Region, and evaluate policy packages for mitigation and climate adaptation.

**Key publications:**
- Impact of congestion on greenhouse gas emissions and urban transport in Mumbai metropolitan region *(Transportation Research Part D, 2017)* — 253 citations
- Impact of rainfall on travel time and fuel usage for Greater Mumbai city *(Transportation Research Procedia, 2020)*
- Impact of past rainfall events on the urban transport sector of Mumbai *(Transportation in Developing Economies, 2020)*

## Carbon Capture & Storage

Research carried out at IIT Delhi and Duke University (2006–2016) focused on the engineering and economics of large-scale CO₂ capture and geological sequestration. This work produced widely cited models for CO₂ trunk pipelines, geosequestration site costs, and the freshwater implications of CCS deployment.

**Key publications:**
- Potential economies of scale in CO₂ transport through use of a trunk pipeline *(Energy Conversion and Management, 2010)* — 178 citations
- Effects of well spacing on geosequestration site costs and surface footprint *(Environmental Science & Technology, 2012)*
- The potential impacts of climate-change policy on freshwater use in thermoelectric power generation *(Energy Policy, 2011)*

---

## Chemical Looping Combustion & Fluidized Bed Engineering

Foundational experimental and simulation work on chemical looping combustion (CLC) — an inherent CO₂-capture combustion technology — using metal oxide oxygen carriers (NiO, CuO) in fluidized bed reactors. Includes design of a 200 MW CLC power plant concept and characterisation of recirculating fluidized bed hydrodynamics.

**Key publications:**
- Experimental investigation of metal oxides for chemical looping combustion *(Fuel, 2009)*
- Biogas combustion in a chemical looping fluidized bed reactor *(International Journal of Greenhouse Gas Control, 2009)*
- 200-MW chemical looping combustion based thermal power plant for clean power generation *(International Journal of Energy Research, 2011)*
- Pressure drop and gas bypassing characteristics in recirculating fluidized beds *(Chemical Engineering Science, 2006)*
